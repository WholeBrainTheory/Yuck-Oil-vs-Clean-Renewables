# Yuck-Oil-vs-Clean-Renewables
A collection of human-made public domain graphics about energy sources for a viable future.

## Origin
I created these graphics initially in response to deflective comments that "CO2 is good for plants" to support predatory delay in reducing carbon-based fuels use. Carbon-based fuels are sequestered carbon that we have been reintroducing to our environment in an unprecedented rate over the last century. Most of these compounds are toxic, produce toxic by-products when refined, cost energy to create and distribute, and release toxic compounds when burnt. This has been led to industrialization on a large scale but the waste products have been accumulating in the environment because the environment cannot process it all.

All living organisms that use carbon compounds in their energy cycle exhaust CO2 as waste, including plants. CO2 is literally shit, a waste by-product that all living organisms expel.

## Why Managing CO2 Is Important
CO2 levels are rising indicating natural CO2 sinks, plants included, are insufficient to combat our CO2 production. Organisms on Earth are adapted to what was historically lower and stable CO2 levels and will not thrive in higher CO2 levels. That includes humans, our food and service animals, and the rest of nature as well. At sufficient levels, CO2 can be directly harmful.
 
The more pressing problem is the energy trapping effects of CO2. CO2 inhibits radiation of solar energy back into space. This traps more energy within the atmosphere, oceans, and land. More energy means higher temperatures and more energetic weather including bigger storms and dramatic changes in local climates leading to shifts in water supply and average temperatures faster than local flora and fauna can adapt. We are currently in a period of mass extinction, with loss of biodiversity and biomass.

Our survival is dependent on reduced CO2, reduced environmental temperature, and maintenance of historical patterns of weather. With rising CO2 levels and the associated changes in our environment, we will not be able to exist outside of highly controlled environments. Earth is the only planet we know of anywhere in the universe where it is possible to thrive without needing technology moment to moment. This is important because technology for life support comes with technical, economic, logistical, and ethical problems that provide considerable friction to human living and development.

This current state was predicted over 100 years ago, confirmed by fossil fuel producing companies 50 years ago, and is a problem for everyone now. CO2 has to be managed aggressively because there is so much of it and production has been exceeding disposal for generations. Calculations of the public cost to health and the environment for each barrel of refined oil are more than the profits gained. The estimated range of the social cost of oil exceeds the recent prices for a barrel of oil. Delaying the transition away from burning carbon-based fuel for energy to non-carbon energy for most uses at this point is predatory.

We are at a pivotal point in human life; we can transition to a post-carbon energy economy or perish in a series of greater and greater then smaller and smaller disasters.

We can have a big bright beautiful tomorrow but it comes from using non-carbon energy sources like solar, wind, tidal, geothermal and nuclear, and not coal, natural gas, or oil. The best time to start was last century. The second best time to start is now.

## About The Graphics
These graphics are designed to present simplified but true messages about our energy sources. They are released into the **public domain** so anyone can produce and remix copies for themselves for personal or commercial use. There are multiple ways to push towoard reduced carbon production and to combat climate change and this is but one of them. Use the graphics to make t-shirts, stickers, bumper stickers, signs, billboards, webpages, apps, posts and presentations. Use them to make money on the way to a post-carbon economy. Use them to make ***positive*** change.

## File Details
Each graphic comes as a Scalable Vector Graphic (SVG) and Portable Network Graphic (PNG) at 300dpi. Many programs can open and manipulate these file formats and they can easily be imported into documents, webpages, apps, presentations and more.

**SVG** files can be modified with the free open source program [Inkscape](https://inkscape.org).

**PNG** files can be modified and modified with the free open source program [Krita](https://kritra.org).
